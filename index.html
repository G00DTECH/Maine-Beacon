Maine's 2025 count shows 281 unsheltered persons facing life-threatening winter conditions. With temperatures plunging to -30째F wind chills and over 156 days below freezing each year, there is no systematic program distributing survival equipment and MREs to the state's unsheltered population.

Our Solution

Meeting people where they are with dignity and life-saving support

Winter Survival Kits

Cold-weather sleeping bags rated to 0째F, emergency blankets, hand and foot warmers, and weatherproof tarps distributed directly to those in need.

MRE Nutrition Program

Shelf-stable, high-calorie meals requiring no preparation, providing 3 days of critical nutrition to sustain individuals in harsh conditions.

Pathways to Housing

Every interaction connects individuals to Maine's Coordinated Entry system, rapid rehousing programs, and permanent housing solutions.

Our Mission

To preserve lives and dignity by providing survival equipment and nourishment to homeless individuals in Maine's harshest conditions, while connecting them to pathways toward permanent housing.

Our Core Values

1

Dignity First

Every interaction treats recipients as neighbors deserving respect, not charity cases. Distribution practices emphasize choice, privacy, and autonomy.

2

Life-Saving Urgency

Maine winters kill. When temperatures threaten survival, we maintain emergency deployment capability for severe weather events.

3

Pathway, Not Endpoint

Survival equipment sustains life; housing restores it. Every kit includes information connecting recipients to housing services.

4

Veteran Commitment

Founded by a veteran, we prioritize reaching homeless veterans who increased 10% in Maine's 2025 count despite overall population decreases.
